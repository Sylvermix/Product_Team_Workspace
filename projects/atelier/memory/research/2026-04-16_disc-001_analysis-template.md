## How to Use This Template

This template has three levels:
1. **Per-participant notes** (one page per session, fill in during/immediately after each interview)
2. **Cross-participant synthesis** (fill in after all 5 sessions are complete)
3. **Decision recommendation** (fill in after synthesis is complete; this is the deliverable)

Do not begin the cross-participant synthesis until all 5 per-participant notes are complete. Do not write the decision recommendation until the synthesis is complete.

The analyst must run the bias checks (section 4) before finalizing the recommendation. The bias check is not optional.

## Section 4: Bias Checks

*The analyst completes this checklist before finalizing the recommendation. Each check requires a written answer, not just a checkbox.*

---

**Bias Check 1: Did we over-weight articulate participants?**

*The most eloquent, confident participant is not necessarily the most representative. Review your evidence grid. Is the synthesis dominated by one or two voices?*

Reflection: _______________

If yes, what is the verdict if you weight the quieter participants equally?

---

**Bias Check 2: Did we confirmation-bias toward the design we already built?**

*We have an onboarding design (journey-map.md, mockup-spec.md) we spent effort on. Did any disconfirming signals get minimized in synthesis because they would require changing that design?*

Reflection: _______________

Specifically: were Stimulus A reactions (the offering screen) analyzed honestly for disconfirming signals, or did we explain them away?

---

**Bias Check 3: Did we conflate "users liked the concept" with "users will use it"?**

*Concept enthusiasm in a research session is notoriously unreliable. Review your per-participant notes. How much of your "supported" evidence is past-behavior evidence vs. expressed enthusiasm?*

Breakdown:
- Past-behavior evidence supporting hypothesis: X / 5 participants
- Expressed enthusiasm only (no behavior anchor): X / 5 participants

If expressed enthusiasm outnumbers behavior evidence, downgrade your confidence level by one step.

---

**Bias Check 4: Did we collapse divergent findings into false consensus?**

*Review Section 2.4 (Divergent Findings). Are there splits that are being underweighted in the synthesis? Check: does the recommendation hold if the divergent group is actually the majority, and the supporters are the outliers?*
